How to Maintain Your Furnace


Your furnace is one of the most important pieces of equipment in your home. It keeps you warm all winter long, and if it breaks down, you’re going to be very uncomfortable. So it’s important to take care of it and make sure that it’s in good working order. Here are a few tips on how to maintain your furnace and keep it running smoothly all winter long:

Check the air filter monthly


The air filter is one of the most important parts of the furnace, and it needs to be checked every month. If it’s dirty, it will restrict airflow and cause the furnace to work harder than it needs to. So check it every month and replace it if necessary.

Another important part of the furnace is the blower motor. This is what moves the air through the system and into your home. If it’s not working properly, the furnace won’t be able to do its job. So make sure to have a professional check it out every year or so to make sure it’s in good working order.

Don’t forget about the ductwork. This is what carries the air from the furnace to all of the different rooms in your home. If it’s not properly maintained, it can cause all sorts of problems, including decreased airflow and increased energy costs. So have it inspected and cleaned regularly by a professional.

Vacuum around the furnace


Another important part of furnace maintenance is to vacuum the unit regularly. This will help to remove any dust or dirt that could potentially get into the unit and cause problems. It is also a good idea to check the furnace filter every month and replace it if it appears to be clogged.

In addition to regular maintenance, it is important to have your furnace serviced by a professional at least once a year. This will help to ensure that your furnace is running properly and that any potential problems are caught early. By having your furnace serviced regularly, you can avoid costly repairs or replacements down the road.

Have your furnace serviced regularly


It’s a good idea to have your furnace serviced by a professional at least once a year. They will be able to clean it and make sure that all of the parts are in good working order. This will help to prolong the life of your furnace and keep it running efficiently.

If you notice that your furnace is not working as well as it used to, then it is probably time for a tune-up. You may also want to consider getting a new furnace if yours is more than 10 years old. Furnaces tend to lose efficiency over time, so a newer model will likely be more energy-efficient.

When shopping for a new furnace, be sure to ask about energy-efficiency ratings. The higher the rating, the more efficient the furnace will be. You should also make sure that the furnace you choose is properly sized for your home. An improperly sized furnace will not work as efficiently and could end up costing you more money in the long run.

Test your furnace regularly


You should test your furnace regularly to make sure that it’s working properly. You can do this by turning it on and setting the thermostat to a low setting for a few minutes. If everything seems to be working fine, then you’re good to go. However, if you notice any strange noises or smells coming from your furnace, then it’s time to call a professional for an inspection.

Another thing you can do to keep your furnace in good shape is to change the air filter regularly. A clogged air filter can impede airflow and cause your furnace to work harder than it needs to. As a result, changing your air filter regularly can prolong the life of your furnace and help it run more efficiently.

Finally, make sure you schedule annual maintenance appointments with a qualified HVAC technician. During these appointments, the technician will clean and inspect your furnace to ensure that it’s in good working condition. They may also spot potential problems and fix them before they become major issues. Annual furnace maintenance is the best way to keep your furnace running smoothly for years to come.

Keep the area around your furnace clear


It’s important to keep the area around your furnace clear so that there’s adequate airflow. If there are too many things around the unit, it could cause it to overheat and break down. In addition, you’ll want to make sure that the furnace is level. An uneven surface can also lead to problems with the unit.

If your furnace isn’t working properly, it’s important to call a professional for help. Trying to fix the problem on your own could end up costing you more in the long run. A professional will be able to quickly diagnose the issue and get your furnace up and running again. When it comes to furnace maintenance, it’s important to have a professional service the unit every year. This will ensure that it’s running properly and that there aren’t any potential problems.

Don’t try to repair your furnace yourself


If something goes wrong with your furnace, don’t try to fix it yourself. It’s best to leave repairs to the professionals so that you don’t end up doing more harm than good. However, there are a few things that you can do to troubleshoot your furnace and possibly fix the problem yourself.

First, check to make sure that your furnace is turned on and that there is power running to it. If everything looks good in this department, then the next thing you should check is the pilot light. If the pilot light is out, then you will need to relight it. Once the pilot light is lit, you should be good to go.

By following these tips, you can help to keep your furnace in good working order and make it last for many years to come. If you have any concerns about your furnace, be sure to contact a professional for help.
